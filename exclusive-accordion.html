Taper

The gradual reduction in training volume and intensity in the weeks leading up to a marathon. This allows the body to recover and store energy for race day.

Carb Loading

The practice of increasing carbohydrate intake in the days before a marathon to maximize glycogen stores in the muscles and liver.

The Wall

A point during the marathon (usually around mile 20) where glycogen stores are depleted, causing extreme fatigue and difficulty maintaining pace.

Tempo Run

A sustained effort run at a "comfortably hard" pace, typically lasting 20-40 minutes. Helps improve lactate threshold and race pace endurance.

Interval Training

Alternating between high-intensity running and recovery periods. Improves speed, cardiovascular fitness, and running economy.

Long Run

The longest run of the week, gradually increasing in distance during training. Builds endurance and prepares the body for the marathon distance.

Recovery Run

A slow, easy-paced run that helps the body recover from harder workouts while maintaining aerobic fitness.

Negative Split

Running the second half of the marathon faster than the first half. A strategic approach that helps conserve energy and finish strong.

Cross Training

Alternative forms of exercise (swimming, cycling, strength training) that complement running and help prevent injury.

Foam Rolling

Self-myofascial release technique using a foam roller to massage muscles and connective tissue, helping with recovery and injury prevention.
